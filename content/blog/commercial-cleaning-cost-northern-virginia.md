If you've called around for commercial cleaning quotes in Northern Virginia, you've probably noticed the numbers are all over the place. One company quotes $400 a month. Another quotes $1,200 for what sounds like the same scope. It's confusing, and most of the pricing pages online don't help.

This guide walks through what commercial cleaning actually costs in Arlington, Alexandria, McLean, Tysons, Falls Church, and the surrounding area in 2026, plus the variables that explain the spread.

## The short answer

For a typical small-to-midsize professional office in Northern Virginia, expect to pay somewhere in these ranges:

| Office Size | Weekly Service | Twice-Weekly Service |
|---|---|---|
| Small (under 1,500 sq ft) | $180 / visit | $160 / visit |
| Medium (1,500 to 3,000 sq ft) | $320 / visit | $290 / visit |
| Large (3,000 to 5,000 sq ft) | $550 / visit | $500 / visit |

Monthly totals usually land between $720 and $4,400 depending on square footage. The frequency of cleaning per week can also provide frequncy discounts, lowering the total monthly cost of cleaning. Those are Capitol Shine's current published rates for standard office environments in Arlington and the rest of NoVA. Other companies in the market price similarly when you normalize scope.

## What actually drives the price

Square footage is the starting point, but four other variables decide where you land in the range.

### 1. Frequency

The more often cleaners are in the space, the lower the per-visit rate. A twice-weekly contract typically runs 10 to 15 percent less per visit than a weekly contract at the same square footage, because the space is easier to maintain when it's been cleaned recently.

If your office runs a hybrid schedule and only has people in Tuesday through Thursday, twice-weekly service is often the sweet spot. Five-day-a-week service is usually overkill unless you're running retail or a medical practice.

### 2. Type of space

A law firm in Rosslyn and a fitness studio in Ballston have the same square footage and completely different cleaning needs. Medical and dental practices require biohazard handling. Restaurants need grease-aware kitchen protocols. Gyms need disinfectant turnover between sessions. Standard office space is the baseline. Anything specialized adds 15 to 40 percent.

### 3. Floor type and layout

Carpet throughout a 3,000 sq ft office is faster to clean than the same office with tile, hardwood, and an LVT kitchen floor. Open-plan layouts clean faster than heavily partitioned spaces with lots of private offices, because vacuuming an open floor is more efficient than navigating around furniture.

### 4. Restrooms and kitchens

These are the two rooms that drive labor time per visit. An office with one small restroom and a break-room sink is quick. An office with two multi-stall restrooms and a full kitchen will add 20 to 30 minutes of labor per visit, which shows up in the price.

## Add-ons you'll see quoted separately

Most commercial contracts price these à la carte rather than bundling them into the recurring rate:

- **Restroom deep clean** (quarterly or monthly): around $50 per restroom
- **Kitchen or break room detail** (monthly): around $40
- **Floor buffing and waxing** (quarterly): around $150 per service
- **Window washing (exterior)**: around $8 per pane

If a cleaning company quotes you a low base rate and no add-ons, ask what's included. Often the first quote excludes restroom deep cleans or floor care, and those get billed separately later.

## What to watch out for in quotes

A few patterns we see when prospects bring us competing bids.

**Hourly quotes with no scope.** "We'll send two cleaners for three hours, $45 an hour." That sounds reasonable until the third week when they're out the door in 90 minutes and still charging full. Fixed-scope pricing is almost always better.

**Unusually low lead-in pricing.** If a quote comes in significantly below the ranges above, there's usually a reason. Either the scope is narrower than you think, the company is underinsured, or the labor is structured in a way you wouldn't want associated with your business. Cheap commercial cleaning has a way of costing more than it saves.

**Long contract lock-ins.** Month-to-month with a 30-day cancellation window is the standard that reputable Northern Virginia commercial cleaners offer in 2026. If a company is pushing you into a 12-month contract with a termination fee, they're protecting themselves against their own service quality.

## How Arlington compares to the rest of NoVA

Pricing across Arlington, Alexandria, McLean, Tysons Corner, Falls Church, Reston, Vienna, and Fairfax is roughly consistent, within about 10 percent. The bigger variable is commute time for crews, which starts to matter for early-morning or late-night shifts in areas further from the 66/395 corridor.

Buildings in Rosslyn, Ballston, Crystal City, Pentagon City, and Courthouse tend to have on-site building management and loading dock requirements that add a bit of coordination overhead. Standalone offices in McLean, Falls Church, or along Columbia Pike are usually simpler to service.

## Getting to a real number for your space

Every quote worth taking seriously starts with a walk-through. Square footage alone isn't enough. A cleaner needs to see the floor plan, the flooring, the restrooms, the kitchen, and how the space actually gets used during the week before giving you a firm price.

A walk-through takes 15 to 20 minutes and it's free. If a company offers to quote your space sight-unseen off a Google Maps photo, that's a sign the number they give you isn't going to hold once they actually show up.
